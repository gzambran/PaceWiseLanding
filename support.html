Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I save a calculation?
After viewing your splits or training paces, tap the menu button (•••) and select Save. Saved calculations can be revisited anytime.
Q: What kind of training paces can I calculate?
PaceWise calculates interval training paces based on your goal time—covering reps from 200m to 2000m. These are great for track sessions and structured speed workouts. Support for tempo, threshold, and long run pacing is coming soon.
Q: Can I log or track workouts in the app?
No—PaceWise is a planning and reference tool. It's designed to help you calculate pacing for workouts and races, not to log completed runs.
Q: What else can I calculate?
You can also calculate race splits, treadmill speed equivalents, equivalent performance times across distances, and age-graded results.
Q: Is PaceWise meant to be used during a run?
No—PaceWise is a planning and reference tool, not a live tracking app. It's designed to help you prepare for your workouts or races by calculating pacing details ahead of time. You can save calculations to review later, but the app is not intended for use while actively running.
Q: Does PaceWise include GPS tracking or sync with other apps?
PaceWise is focused purely on helping you calculate and reference pace-related data. We don't integrate with GPS or fitness platforms like Strava, Garmin, or Apple Health.
Q: Can I use custom distances or rep lengths?
Currently, interval training paces are provided for standard rep distances (200m to 2000m), and race splits can be viewed in miles, kilometers, 5Ks, and 5-mile chunks. Support for more flexible/custom intervals is something we're exploring for future updates.
